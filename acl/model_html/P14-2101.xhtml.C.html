[0] Latent topics derived by topic models such as Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) are the result of hidden thematic structures which provide further insights into the data. [1] The automatic labelling of such topics derived from social media poses however new challenges since topics may characterise novel events happening in the real world. [2] Existing automatic topic labelling approaches which depend on external knowledge sources become less applicable here since relevant articles/concepts of the extracted topics may not exist in external sources. [3] In this paper we propose to address the problem of automatic labelling of latent topics learned from Twitter as a summarisation problem. [4] We introduce a framework which apply summarisation algorithms to generate topic labels. [5] These algorithms are independent of external sources and only rely on the identification of dominant terms in documents related to the latent topic. [6] We compare the efficiency of existing state of the art summarisation algorithms. [7] Our results suggest that summarisation algorithms generate better topic labels which capture event-related context compared to the top- n terms returned by LDA. [8] In this paper we proposed a novel alternative to topic labelling which do not rely on external data sources. [9] To the best of out knowledge no existing work has been formally studied for automatic labelling through summarisation. [10] This experiment shows that existing summarisation techniques can be exploited to provide a better label of a topic, extending in this way a topic s information by providing a richer context than top-terms. [11] These results show that there is room to further improve upon existing summarisation techniques to cater for generating candidate labels.
