[1] Much of the recent work on dependency parsing has been focused on solving inherent combinatorial problems associated with rich scoring functions.. [2] In contrast, we demonstrate that highly expressive scoring functions can be used with substantially simpler inference procedures.. [3] Specifically, we introduce a sampling-based parser that can easily handle arbitrary global features.. [4] Inspired by SampleRank, we learn to take guided stochastic steps towards a high scoring parse.. [5] We introduce two samplers for traversing the space of trees, Gibbs and Metropolis-Hastings with Random Walk.. [6] The model outperforms state-of-the-art results when evaluated on 14 languages of non-projective CoNLL datasets.. [7] Our sampling-based approach naturally extends to joint prediction scenarios, such as joint parsing and POS correction.. [8] The resulting method outperforms the best reported results on the CATiB dataset, approaching performance of parsing with gold tags..
