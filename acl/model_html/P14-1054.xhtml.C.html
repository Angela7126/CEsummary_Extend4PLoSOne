[0] Chinese is an ancient hieroglyphic. [1] It is inattentive to structure. [2] Therefore, segmenting and parsing Chinese are more difficult and less accurate. [3] In this paper, we propose an Omni-word feature and a soft constraint method for Chinese relation extraction. [4] The Omni-word feature uses every potential word in a sentence as lexicon feature, reducing errors caused by word segmentation. [5] In order to utilize the structure information of a relation instance, we discuss how soft constraint can be used to capture the local dependency. [6] Both Omni-word feature and soft constraint make a better use of sentence information and minimize the influences caused by Chinese word segmentation and parsing. [7] We test these methods on the ACE 2005 RDC Chinese corpus. [8] The results show a significant improvement in Chinese relation extraction, outperforming other methods in F-score by 10% in 6 relation types and 15% in 18 relation subtypes. [9] In this paper, We proposed a novel Omni-word feature taking advantages of Chinese sub-phrases. [10] We also introduced the soft constraint method for Chinese relation recognition. [11] The soft constraint utilizes four constraint conditions to catch the structure information in a relation instance. [12] Both the Omni-word feature and soft constrain make better use of information a sentence has, and minimize the deficiency caused by Chinese segmentation and parsing. [13] The size of the employed lexicon determines the dimension of the feature space. [14] The first impression is that more lexicon entries result in more power. [15] However, more lexicon entries also increase the computational complexity and bring in noises. [16] In our future work, we will study this issue. [17] The notion of soft constraints can also be extended to include more patterns, rules, regexes or syntactic constraints that have been used for information extraction. [18] The usability of these strategies is also left for future work.
