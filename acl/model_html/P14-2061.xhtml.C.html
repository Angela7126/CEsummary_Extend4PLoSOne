[0] Prior research on language identification focused primarily on text and speech. [1] In this paper, we focus on the visual modality and present a method for identifying sign languages solely from short video samples. [2] The method is trained on unlabelled video data (unsupervised feature learning) and using these features, it is trained to discriminate between six sign languages (supervised learning. [3] We ran experiments on short video samples involving 30 signers (about 6 hours in total. [4] Using leave-one-signer-out cross-validation, our evaluation shows an average best accuracy of 84 %. [5] Given that sign languages are under-resourced, unsupervised feature learning techniques are the right tools and our results indicate that this is realistic for sign language identification. [9] In our experiments, we used a single hidden layer of features, but it is worth researching into deeper layers to improve performance and gain more insight into the hierarchical composition of features.
