[1] Automatically detecting verbal irony (roughly, sarcasm) is a challenging task because ironists say something other than and often opposite to what they actually mean.. [2] Discerning ironic intent exclusively from the words and syntax comprising texts (e.g.,, tweets, forum posts) is therefore not always possible additional contextual information about the speaker and/or the topic at hand is often necessary.. [3] We introduce a new corpus that provides empirical evidence for this claim.. [4] We show that annotators frequently require context to make judgements concerning ironic intent, and that machine learning approaches tend to misclassify those same comments for which annotators required additional context..
