[1] Finite-state chunking and tagging methods are very fast for annotating non-hierarchical syntactic information, and are often applied in applications that do not require full syntactic analyses.. [2] Scenarios such as incremental machine translation may benefit from some degree of hierarchical syntactic analysis without requiring fully connected parses.. [3] We introduce hedge parsing as an approach to recovering constituents of length up to some maximum span L.. [4] This approach improves efficiency by bounding constituent size, and allows for efficient segmentation strategies prior to parsing.. [5] Unlike shallow parsing methods, hedge parsing yields internal hierarchical structure of phrases within its span bound.. [6] We present the approach and some initial experiments on different inference strategies..
