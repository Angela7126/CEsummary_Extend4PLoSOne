[1] Large-scale knowledge bases are important assets in NLP.. [2] Frequently, such resources are constructed through automatic mergers of complementary resources, such as WordNet and Wikipedia.. [3] However, manually validating these resources is prohibitively expensive, even when using methods such as crowdsourcing.. [4] We propose a cost-effective method of validating and extending knowledge bases using video games with a purpose.. [5] Two video games were created to validate concept-concept and concept-image relations.. [6] In experiments comparing with crowdsourcing, we show that video game-based validation consistently leads to higher-quality annotations, even when players are not compensated..
