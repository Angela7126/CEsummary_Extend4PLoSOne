[1] This work focuses on two important aspects of answer reranking for non-factoid QA similarity between question and answer content, and answer structure.. [2] While the former has been addressed with a variety of lexical-semantic models, the latter has received little attention.. [3] Here we show how to model answer structures using discourse and how to integrate the two aspects into a holistic framework.. [4] Empirically we show that modeling answer discourse structures is complementary to modeling lexical semantic similarity and that the best performance is obtained when they are tightly integrated.. [5] We evaluate the proposed approach on multiple genres and question types and obtain benefits of up to 24% relative improvement over a strong baseline that combines information retrieval and lexical semantics.. [6] We further demonstrate that answer discourse structures are largely independent of domain and transfer well, even between radically different datasets.. [7] This work is open source and available at http://nlp.sista.arizona.edu/releases/acl2014..
