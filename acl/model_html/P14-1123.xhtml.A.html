[1] Designing measures that capture various aspects of language ability is a central task in the design of systems for automatic scoring of spontaneous speech.. [2] In this study, we address a key aspect of language proficiency assessment syntactic complexity.. [3] We propose a novel measure of syntactic complexity for spontaneous speech that shows optimum empirical performance on real world data in multiple ways.. [4] First, it is both robust and reliable , producing automatic scores that agree well with human rating compared to the state-of-the-art.. [5] Second, the measure makes sense theoretically, both from algorithmic and native language acquisition points of view..
