[0] In this paper, we investigate various strategies to predict both syntactic dependency parsing and contiguous multiword expression (MWE) recognition, testing them on the dependency version of French Treebank [ 1 ] , as instantiated in the SPMRL Shared Task [ 23 ]. [1] Our work focuses on using an alternative representation of syntactically regular MWEs, which captures their syntactic internal structure. [2] We obtain a system with comparable performance to that of previous works on this dataset, but which predicts both syntactic dependencies and the internal structure of MWEs. [3] This can be useful for capturing the various degrees of semantic compositionality of MWEs. [4] We experimented strategies to predict both MWE analysis and dependency structure, and tested them on the dependency version of French Treebank [ 1 ] , as instantiated in the SPMRL Shared Task [ 23 ]. [5] Our work focused on using an alternative representation of syntactically regular MWEs, which captures their syntactic internal structure. [6] We obtain a system with comparable performance to that of previous works on this dataset, but which predicts both syntactic dependencies and the internal structure of MWEs. [7] This can be useful for capturing the various degrees of semantic compositionality of MWEs. [8] The main weakness of our system comes from the identification of regular MWEs, a property which is highly lexical. [9] Our current use of external lexicons does not seem to suffice, and the use of data-driven external information to better cope with this identification can be envisaged.
