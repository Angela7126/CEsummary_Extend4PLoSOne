[1] Medical record retrieval is an important domain-specific IR problem.. [2] Concept-based representation is an effective approach to dealing with ambiguity terminology in medical domain.. [3] However, the results of the NLP tools used to generate the concept-based representation are often not perfect.. [4] In this paper, we present a general methodology that can use axiomatic approaches as guidance to regularize the concept weighting strategies to address the limitations caused by the inaccurate concept mapping and improve the retrieval performance.. [5] In particular, we proposed two weighting regularization methods based on the relations among concepts.. [6] Experimental results show that the proposed methods can significantly outperform existing retrieval functions.. [7] There are many interesting directions for our future work.. [8] First, we plan to study how to automatically predict whether to use concept-based indexing based on the quality of MetaMap results, and explore whether the proposed methods are applicable for other entity linking methods.. [9] Second, we will study how to leverage other information from knowledge bases to further improve the performance.. [10] Third, more experiments could be conducted to examine the effectiveness of the proposed methods when using other ranking strategies.. [11] Finally, it would be interesting to study how to follow the proposed methodology to study other domain-specific IR problems..
