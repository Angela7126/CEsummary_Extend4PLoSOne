[1] This paper aims at extracting new sentiment words from large-scale user-generated content. [2] We propose a fully unsupervised, purely data-driven framework for this purpose. [3] We design statistical measures respectively to quantify the utility of a lexical pattern and to measure the possibility of a word being a new word. [4] The method is almost free of linguistic resources (except POS tags), and requires no elaborated linguistic rules. [5] We also demonstrate how new sentiment word will benefit sentiment analysis. [8] In order to extract new sentiment words from large-scale user-generated content, this paper proposes a fully unsupervised, purely data-driven, and almost knowledge-free (except POS tags) framework. [9] We design statistical measures to quantify the utility of a lexical pattern and to measure the possibility of a word being a new word, respectively. [10] The method is almost free of linguistic resources (except POS tags), and does not rely on elaborated linguistic rules. [11] We conduct extensive experiments to reveal the influence of different statistical measures in new word finding. [12] Comparative experiments show that our proposed method outperforms baselines remarkably. [13] Experiments also demonstrate that inclusion of new sentiment words benefits sentiment classification definitely. [14] From linguistic perspectives, our framework is capable to extract adjective new words because the lexical patterns usually modify adjective words. [15] As future work, we are considering how to extract other types of new sentiment words, such as nounal new words that can express sentiment.
