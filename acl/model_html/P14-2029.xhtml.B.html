[3] This is the most realistic evaluation of methods for predicting sentence-level grammaticality to date.. [4] Surprisingly, the system from Post ( 2011 ) performed quite poorly on the GUG dataset.. [5] We speculate that this is due to the fact that the Post system relies heavily on features extracted from automatic syntactic parses.. [6] While Post found that such a system can effectively distinguish grammatical news text sentences from sentences generated by a language model, measuring the grammaticality of real sentences from language learners seems to require a wider variety of features, including n -gram counts, language model scores, etc.. [7] Of course, our findings do not indicate that syntactic features such as those from Post ( 2011 ) are without value..
