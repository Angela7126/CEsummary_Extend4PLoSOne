[0] In this study, we analyze links between edits in Wikipedia articles and turns from their discussion page. [1] Our motivation is to better understand implicit details about the writing process and knowledge flow in collaboratively created resources. [2] Based on properties of the involved edit and turn, we have defined constraints for corresponding edit-turn-pairs. [3] We manually annotated a corpus of 636 corresponding and non-corresponding edit-turn-pairs. [4] Furthermore, we show how our data can be used to automatically identify corresponding edit-turn-pairs. [5] With the help of supervised machine learning, we achieve an accuracy of .87 for this task. [6] The novelty of this paper is a computational analysis of the relationship between the edit history and the discussion of a Wikipedia article. [7] As far as we are aware, this is the first study to automatically analyze this relationship involving the textual content of edits and turns. [8] Based on the types of turn and edit in an edit-turn-pair, we have operationalized the notion of corresponding and non-corresponding edit-turn-pairs. [9] The basic assumption is that in a corresponding pair, the turn contains an explicit performative and the edit corresponds to this performative. [10] We have presented a machine learning system to automatically detect corresponding edit-turn-pairs. [11] To test this system, we manually annotated a corpus of corresponding and non-corresponding edit-turn-pairs. [12] Trained and tested on this data, our system shows a significant improvement over the baseline. [13] With regard to future work, an extension of the manually annotated corpus is the most important issue. [14] Our classifier can be used to bootstrap the annotation of additional edit-turn-pairs.
