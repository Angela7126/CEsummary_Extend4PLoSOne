[1] We have proposed STRUCT, a general-purpose natural language generation system which is comparable to current state-of-the-art generators.. [2] STRUCT formalizes the generation problem as an MDP and applies a version of the UCT algorithm, a fast online MDP planner, to solve it.. [3] Thus, STRUCT naturally handles probabilistic grammars.. [4] We demonstrate empirically that STRUCT is anytime, comparable to existing generation-as-planning systems in certain NLG tasks, and is also capable of handling other, more complex tasks such as negated communicative goals.. [5] Though STRUCT has many interesting properties, many directions for exploration remain.. [6] Among other things, it would be desirable to integrate STRUCT with discourse planning and dialog systems.. [7] Fortunately, reinforcement learning has already been investigated in such contexts [ 13 ] , indicating that an MDP-based generation procedure could be a natural fit in more complex generation systems.. [8] This is a primary direction for future work.. [9] A second direction is that, due to the nature of the approach, STRUCT is highly amenable to parallelization.. [10] None of the experiments reported here use parallelization, however, to be fair to CRISP.. [11] We plan to parallelize STRUCT in future work, to take advantage of current multicore architectures.. [12] This should obviously further reduce generation time.. [13] STRUCT is open source and available from github.com upon request..
