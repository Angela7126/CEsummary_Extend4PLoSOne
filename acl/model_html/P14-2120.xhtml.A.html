[1] We investigate recognizing implied predicate-argument relationships which are not explicitly expressed in syntactic structure.. [2] While prior works addressed such relationships as an extension to semantic role labeling, our work investigates them in the context of textual inference scenarios.. [3] Such scenarios provide prior information, which substantially eases the task.. [4] We provide a large and freely available evaluation dataset for our task setting, and propose methods to cope with it, while obtaining promising results in empirical evaluations..
