[1] Multi-document summarization (MDS) systems have been designed for short, unstructured summaries of 10-15 documents, and are inadequate for larger document collections.. [2] We propose a new approach to scaling up summarization called hierarchical summarization , and present the first implemented system, Summa.. [3] Summa produces a hierarchy of relatively short summaries, in which the top level provides a general overview and users can navigate the hierarchy to drill down for more details on topics of interest.. [4] Summa optimizes for coherence as well as coverage of salient information.. [5] In an Amazon Mechanical Turk evaluation, users prefered Summa ten times as often as flat MDS and three times as often as timelines..
