[1] Statistical Machine Translation (SMT) usually utilizes contextual information to disambiguate translation candidates.. [2] However, it is often limited to contexts within sentence boundaries, hence broader topical information cannot be leveraged.. [3] In this paper, we propose a novel approach to learning topic representation for parallel data using a neural network architecture, where abundant topical contexts are embedded via topic relevant monolingual data.. [4] By associating each translation rule with the topic representation, topic relevant rules are selected according to the distributional similarity with the source text during SMT decoding.. [5] Experimental results show that our method significantly improves translation accuracy in the NIST Chinese-to-English translation task compared to a state-of-the-art baseline..
