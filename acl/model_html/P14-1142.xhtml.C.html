[0] It is very import for Chinese language processing with the aid of an efficient input method engine (IME), of which pinyin-to-Chinese (PTC) conversion is the core part. [1] Meanwhile, though typos are inevitable during user pinyin inputting, existing IMEs paid little attention to such big inconvenience. [2] In this paper, motivated by a key equivalence of two decoding algorithms, we propose a joint graph model to globally optimize PTC and typo correction for IME. [3] The evaluation results show that the proposed method outperforms both existing academic and commercial IMEs. [4] In this paper, we have developed a joint graph model for pinyin-to-Chinese conversion with typo correction. [5] This model finds a joint global optimal for typo correction and PTC conversion on the entire input pinyin sequence. [6] The evaluation results show that our model outperforms both previous academic systems and existing commercial products. [7] In addition, the joint model is efficient enough for practical use.
