[0] In this paper, we report our preliminary efforts in building an English-Turkish parallel treebank corpus for statistical machine translation. [1] In the corpus, we manually generated parallel trees for about 5,000 sentences from Penn Treebank. [2] English sentences in our set have a maximum of 15 tokens, including punctuation. [3] We constrained the translated trees to the reordering of the children and the replacement of the leaf nodes with appropriate glosses. [4] We also report the tools that we built and used in our tree translation task. [5] Parallel treebank construction efforts increased significantly in the recent years. [6] Many parallel treebanks are produced to build statistically strong language models for different languages. [7] In this study, we report our preliminary efforts to build such a parallel corpus for Turkish-English pair. [8] We translated and transformed a subset of parse trees of Penn Treebank to Turkish. [9] We cover more than 50% of all sentences with a maximum length of 15-words including punctuation. [10] This work constitutes the preliminary step of parallel treebank generation. [11] As a next step, we will focus on morphological analysis and disambiguation of Turkish words. [12] After determining the correct morphological analysis of Turkish words, we will use the parts of these analyses to replace the leaf nodes that we intentionally left as *NONE* . [13] As a future work, we plan to expand the dataset to include all Penn Treebank sentences.
