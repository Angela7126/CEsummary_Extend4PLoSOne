[1] In linguistic annotation projects, we typically develop annotation guidelines to minimize disagreement.. [2] However, in this position paper we question whether we should actually limit the disagreements between annotators, rather than embracing them.. [3] We present an empirical analysis of part-of-speech annotated data sets that suggests that disagreements are systematic across domains and to a certain extend also across languages.. [4] This points to an underlying ambiguity rather than random errors.. [5] Moreover, a quantitative analysis of tag confusions reveals that the majority of disagreements are due to linguistically debatable cases rather than annotation errors..
