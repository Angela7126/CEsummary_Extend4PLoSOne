[1] Results of Phase 1 provide support for the Semantic Consistency Hypothesis, at least as a strong bias.. [2] More work will be needed to determine the strength of that bias.. [3] The findings are largely consistent with VerbNet s semantics, but changes are indicated in some cases.. [4] We find that inter-annotator agreement is sufficiently high that annotation can be done effectively using the modal response with an average of 6-7 responses per item.. [5] We are currently investigating whether we can achieve better reliability with fewer responses per item by taking into account an individual annotator s history across items, as recent work suggests is possible [ 13 , 17 , 19 ].. [6] Thus, crowd-sourcing VerbNet semantic entailments appears to be both feasible and productive.. [7] Data-collection continues.. [8] Phase 2, which added over 10 new verb classes, is complete.. [9] Phase 3, which includes both new classes and new entailments, has been launched..
