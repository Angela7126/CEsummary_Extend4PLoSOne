[0] Named Entity Disambiguation (NED) refers to the task of mapping different named entity mentions in running text to their correct interpretations in a specific knowledge base (KB. [1] This paper presents a collective disambiguation approach using a graph model. [2] All possible NE candidates are represented as nodes in the graph and associations between different candidates are represented by edges between the nodes. [3] Each node has an initial confidence score, e.g., entity popularity. [4] Page-Rank is used to rank nodes and the final rank is combined with the initial confidence for candidate selection. [5] Experiments on 27,819 NE textual mentions show the effectiveness of using Page-Rank in conjunction with initial confidence. [6] 87% accuracy is achieved, outperforming both baseline and state-of-the-art approaches. [7] Our results show that Page-Rank in conjunction with re-ranking by initial confidence score can be used as an effective approach to collectively disambiguate named entity textual mentions in a document. [8] Our proposed features are very simple and easy to extract, and work well when employed in PR. [9] In future work we plan to explore enriching the edges between nodes by incorporating semantic relations extracted from an ontology.
