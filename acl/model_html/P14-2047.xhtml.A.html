[1] We present a study of aspects of discourse structure specifically discourse devices used to organize information in a sentence that significantly impact the quality of machine translation.. [2] Our analysis is based on manual evaluations of translations of news from Chinese and Arabic to English.. [3] We find that there is a particularly strong mismatch in the notion of what constitutes a sentence in Chinese and English, which occurs often and is associated with significant degradation in translation quality.. [4] Also related to lower translation quality is the need to employ multiple explicit discourse connectives ( because, but , etc.), as well as the presence of ambiguous discourse connectives in the English translation.. [5] Furthermore, the mismatches between discourse expressions across languages significantly impact translation quality..
