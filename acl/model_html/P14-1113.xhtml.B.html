[1] This paper proposes a novel method for semantic hierarchy construction based on word embeddings, which are trained using a large-scale corpus.. [2] Using the word embeddings, we learn the hypernym hyponym relationship by estimating projection matrices which map words to their hypernyms.. [3] Further improvements are made using a cluster-based approach in order to model the more fine-grained relations.. [4] Then we propose a few simple criteria to identity whether a new word pair is a hypernym hyponym relation.. [5] Based on the pairwise hypernym hyponym relations, we build semantic hierarchies automatically.. [6] In our experiments, the proposed method significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods and achieves the best F1-score of 73.74% on a manually labeled test dataset.. [7] Further experiments show that our method is complementary to the previous manually-built hierarchy extension methods.. [8] For future work, we aim to improve word embedding learning under the guidance of hypernym hyponym relations.. [9] By including the hypernym hyponym relation constraints while training word embeddings, we expect to improve the embeddings such that they become more suitable for this task..
