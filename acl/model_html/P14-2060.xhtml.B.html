[1] In this paper we have presented methods for high precision abbreviation expansion for a TTS application.. [2] The methods are largely self-organizing, using in-domain unannotated data, and depend on only a small amount of annotated data.. [3] Since the SVM features relate to general properties of abbreviations, expansions and contexts, the classifier parameters will likely carry over to new (English) domains.. [4] We demonstrate that in combination with a hand-built TTS baseline, the methods afford dramatic improvement in the TP rate (to about 74% from a starting point of about 40%) and a reduction of FP to below our goal of 2%..
