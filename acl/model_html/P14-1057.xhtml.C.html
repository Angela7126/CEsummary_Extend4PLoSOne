[0] An important search task in the biomedical domain is to find medical records of patients who are qualified for a clinical trial. [1] One commonly used approach is to apply NLP tools to map terms from queries and documents to concepts and then compute the relevance scores based on the concept-based representation. [2] However, the mapping results are not perfect, and none of previous work studied how to deal with them in the retrieval process. [3] In this paper, we focus on addressing the limitations caused by the imperfect mapping results and study how to further improve the retrieval performance of the concept-based ranking methods. [4] In particular, we apply axiomatic approaches and propose two weighting regularization methods that adjust the weighting based on the relations among the concepts. [5] Experimental results show that the proposed methods are effective to improve the retrieval performance, and their performances are comparable to other top-performing systems in the TREC Medical Records Track. [6] Medical record retrieval is an important domain-specific IR problem. [7] Concept-based representation is an effective approach to dealing with ambiguity terminology in medical domain. [8] However, the results of the NLP tools used to generate the concept-based representation are often not perfect. [9] In this paper, we present a general methodology that can use axiomatic approaches as guidance to regularize the concept weighting strategies to address the limitations caused by the inaccurate concept mapping and improve the retrieval performance. [10] In particular, we proposed two weighting regularization methods based on the relations among concepts. [11] Experimental results show that the proposed methods can significantly outperform existing retrieval functions. [12] There are many interesting directions for our future work. [13] First, we plan to study how to automatically predict whether to use concept-based indexing based on the quality of MetaMap results, and explore whether the proposed methods are applicable for other entity linking methods. [14] Second, we will study how to leverage other information from knowledge bases to further improve the performance. [15] Third, more experiments could be conducted to examine the effectiveness of the proposed methods when using other ranking strategies. [16] Finally, it would be interesting to study how to follow the proposed methodology to study other domain-specific IR problems.
