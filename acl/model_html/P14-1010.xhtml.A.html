[1] Morphological segmentation is an effective sparsity reduction strategy for statistical machine translation (SMT) involving morphologically complex languages.. [2] When translating into a segmented language, an extra step is required to desegment the output; previous studies have desegmented the 1-best output from the decoder.. [3] In this paper, we expand our translation options by desegmenting n -best lists or lattices.. [4] Our novel lattice desegmentation algorithm effectively combines both segmented and desegmented views of the target language for a large subspace of possible translation outputs, which allows for inclusion of features related to the desegmentation process, as well as an unsegmented language model (LM.. [5] We investigate this technique in the context of English-to-Arabic and English-to-Finnish translation, showing significant improvements in translation quality over desegmentation of 1-best decoder outputs..
