[1] Negation words, such as no and not , play a fundamental role in modifying sentiment of textual expressions.. [2] We will refer to a negation word as the negator and the text span within the scope of the negator as the argument.. [3] Commonly used heuristics to estimate the sentiment of negated expressions rely simply on the sentiment of argument (and not on the negator or the argument itself.. [4] We use a sentiment treebank to show that these existing heuristics are poor estimators of sentiment.. [5] We then modify these heuristics to be dependent on the negators and show that this improves prediction.. [6] Next, we evaluate a recently proposed composition model [] that relies on both the negator and the argument.. [7] This model learns the syntax and semantics of the negator s argument with a recursive neural network.. [8] We show that this approach performs better than those mentioned above.. [9] In addition, we explicitly incorporate the prior sentiment of the argument and observe that this information can help reduce fitting errors..
