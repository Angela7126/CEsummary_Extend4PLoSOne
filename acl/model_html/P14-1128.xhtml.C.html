[0] This study investigates on building a better Chinese word segmentation model for statistical machine translation. [1] It aims at leveraging word boundary information, automatically learned by bilingual character-based alignments, to induce a preferable segmentation model. [2] We propose dealing with the induced word boundaries as soft constraints to bias the continuous learning of a supervised CRFs model, trained by the treebank data (labeled), on the bilingual data (unlabeled. [3] The induced word boundary information is encoded as a graph propagation constraint. [4] The constrained model induction is accomplished by using posterior regularization algorithm. [5] The experiments on a Chinese-to-English machine translation task reveal that the proposed model can bring positive segmentation effects to translation quality. [6] This paper proposed a novel CWS model for the SMT task. [7] This model aims to maintain the linguistic segmentation supervisions from treebank data and simultaneously integrate useful bilingual segmentations induced from the bitexts. [8] This objective is accomplished by three main steps. [9] 1) learn word boundaries from character-based alignments; 2) encode the learned word boundaries into a GP constraint; and 3) training a CRFs model, under the GP constraint, by using the PR framework. [10] The empirical results indicate that the proposed model can yield better segmentations for SMT.
