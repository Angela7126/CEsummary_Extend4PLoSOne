[1] The novelty of this paper is a computational analysis of the relationship between the edit history and the discussion of a Wikipedia article.. [2] As far as we are aware, this is the first study to automatically analyze this relationship involving the textual content of edits and turns.. [3] Based on the types of turn and edit in an edit-turn-pair, we have operationalized the notion of corresponding and non-corresponding edit-turn-pairs.. [4] The basic assumption is that in a corresponding pair, the turn contains an explicit performative and the edit corresponds to this performative.. [5] We have presented a machine learning system to automatically detect corresponding edit-turn-pairs.. [6] To test this system, we manually annotated a corpus of corresponding and non-corresponding edit-turn-pairs.. [7] Trained and tested on this data, our system shows a significant improvement over the baseline.. [8] With regard to future work, an extension of the manually annotated corpus is the most important issue.. [9] Our classifier can be used to bootstrap the annotation of additional edit-turn-pairs..
