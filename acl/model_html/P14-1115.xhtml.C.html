[0] We propose a novel abstractive query-based summarization system for conversations, where queries are defined as phrases reflecting a user information needs. [1] We rank and extract the utterances in a conversation based on the overall content and the phrasal query information. [2] We cluster the selected sentences based on their lexical similarity and aggregate the sentences in each cluster by means of a word graph model. [3] We propose a ranking strategy to select the best path in the constructed graph as a query-based abstract sentence for each cluster. [4] A resulting summary consists of abstractive sentences representing the phrasal query information and the overall content of the conversation. [5] Automatic and manual evaluation results over meeting, chat and email conversations show that our approach significantly outperforms baselines and previous extractive models. [6] Our phrasal query abstraction framework generates a grammatical abstract from a conversation following three steps, as shown in Figure 1. [7] In this phase, our goal is to generate understandable informative abstract sentences that capture the content of the source sentences and represents the information needs defined by queries. [8] There are several ways of generating abstract sentences (e.g., [ 2 , 18 , 8 , 23 ] ); however, most of them rely heavily on the sentence structure. [9] We believe that such approaches are suboptimal, especially in dealing with conversational data, because multiparty written conversations are often poorly structured. [10] Instead, we apply an approach that does not rely on syntax, nor on a standard NLG architecture. [11] Moreover, since dealing with user queries efficiency is an important aspect, we aim for an approach that is also motivated by the speed with which the abstracts are obtained. [12] We perform the task of abstract generation in three steps, as follows. [13] We have presented an unsupervised framework for abstractive summarization of spoken and written conversations based on phrasal queries. [14] For content selection, we propose a sentence extraction model that incorporates query relevance and content importance into the extraction process. [15] For the generation phase, we propose a ranking strategy which selects the best path in the constructed word graph based on fluency, query relevance and content. [16] Both automatic and manual evaluation of our model show substantial improvement over extraction-based methods, including Biased LexRank, which is considered a state-of-the-art system. [17] Moreover, our system also yields good grammaticality score for human evaluation and achieves comparable scores with the original sentences. [18] Our future work is four-fold. [19] First, we are trying to improve our model by incorporating conversational features (e.g.,, speech acts. [20] Second, we aim at implementing a strategy to order the clusters for generating more coherent abstracts. [21] Third, we try to improve our generated summary by resolving coreferences and incorporating speaker information (e.g.,, names) in the clustering and sentence generation phases. [22] Finally, we plan to take advantage of topic shifts to better segment the relevant parts of conversations in relation to phrasal queries.
