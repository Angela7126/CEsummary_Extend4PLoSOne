[0] We study the problem of generating an English sentence given an underlying probabilistic grammar, a world and a communicative goal. [1] We model the generation problem as a Markov decision process with a suitably defined reward function that reflects the communicative goal. [2] We then use probabilistic planning to solve the MDP and generate a sentence that, with high probability, accomplishes the communicative goal. [3] We show empirically that our approach can generate complex sentences with a speed that generally matches or surpasses the state of the art. [4] Further, we show that our approach is anytime and can handle complex communicative goals, including negated goals. [5] We have proposed STRUCT, a general-purpose natural language generation system which is comparable to current state-of-the-art generators. [6] STRUCT formalizes the generation problem as an MDP and applies a version of the UCT algorithm, a fast online MDP planner, to solve it. [7] Thus, STRUCT naturally handles probabilistic grammars. [8] We demonstrate empirically that STRUCT is anytime, comparable to existing generation-as-planning systems in certain NLG tasks, and is also capable of handling other, more complex tasks such as negated communicative goals. [9] Though STRUCT has many interesting properties, many directions for exploration remain. [10] Among other things, it would be desirable to integrate STRUCT with discourse planning and dialog systems. [11] Fortunately, reinforcement learning has already been investigated in such contexts [ 13 ] , indicating that an MDP-based generation procedure could be a natural fit in more complex generation systems. [12] This is a primary direction for future work. [13] A second direction is that, due to the nature of the approach, STRUCT is highly amenable to parallelization. [14] None of the experiments reported here use parallelization, however, to be fair to CRISP. [15] We plan to parallelize STRUCT in future work, to take advantage of current multicore architectures. [16] This should obviously further reduce generation time. [17] STRUCT is open source and available from github.com upon request.
