[1] We replace the overlap mechanism of the Lesk algorithm with a simple, general-purpose Naive Bayes model that measures many-to-many association between two sets of random variables.. [2] Even with simple probability estimates such as maximum likelihood, the model gains significant improvement over the Lesk algorithm on word sense disambiguation tasks.. [3] With additional lexical knowledge from WordNet, performance is further improved to surpass the state-of-the-art results..
