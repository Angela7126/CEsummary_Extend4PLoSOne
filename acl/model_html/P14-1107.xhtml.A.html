[1] Crowdsourcing is a viable mechanism for creating training data for machine translation.. [2] It provides a low cost, fast turn-around way of processing large volumes of data.. [3] However, when compared to professional translation, naive collection of translations from non-professionals yields low-quality results.. [4] Careful quality control is necessary for crowdsourcing to work well.. [5] In this paper, we examine the challenges of a two-step collaboration process with translation and post-editing by non-professionals.. [6] We develop graph-based ranking models that automatically select the best output from multiple redundant versions of translations and edits, and improves translation quality closer to professionals..
