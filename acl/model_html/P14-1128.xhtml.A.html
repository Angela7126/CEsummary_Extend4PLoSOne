[1] This study investigates on building a better Chinese word segmentation model for statistical machine translation.. [2] It aims at leveraging word boundary information, automatically learned by bilingual character-based alignments, to induce a preferable segmentation model.. [3] We propose dealing with the induced word boundaries as soft constraints to bias the continuous learning of a supervised CRFs model, trained by the treebank data (labeled), on the bilingual data (unlabeled.. [4] The induced word boundary information is encoded as a graph propagation constraint.. [5] The constrained model induction is accomplished by using posterior regularization algorithm.. [6] The experiments on a Chinese-to-English machine translation task reveal that the proposed model can bring positive segmentation effects to translation quality..
