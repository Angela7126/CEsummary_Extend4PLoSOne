[1] In this paper, we show that disagreements between professional or lay annotators are systematic and consistent across domains and some of them are systematic also across languages.. [2] In addition, we present an empirical analysis of POS annotations showing that the vast majority of inter-annotator disagreements are competing, but valid, linguistic interpretations.. [3] We propose to embrace such disagreements rather than using annotation guidelines to optimize inter-annotator agreement, which would bias our models in favor of some linguistic theory..
