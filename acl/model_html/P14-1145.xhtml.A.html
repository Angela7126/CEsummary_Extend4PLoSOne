[1] We introduce ConnotationWordNet , a connotation lexicon over the network of words in conjunction with senses.. [2] We formulate the lexicon induction problem as collective inference over pairwise-Markov Random Fields, and present a loopy belief propagation algorithm for inference.. [3] The key aspect of our method is that it is the first unified approach that assigns the polarity of both word- and sense-level connotations, exploiting the innate bipartite graph structure encoded in WordNet.. [4] We present comprehensive evaluation to demonstrate the quality and utility of the resulting lexicon in comparison to existing connotation and sentiment lexicons..
