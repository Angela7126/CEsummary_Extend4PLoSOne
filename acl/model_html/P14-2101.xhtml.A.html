[1] Latent topics derived by topic models such as Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) are the result of hidden thematic structures which provide further insights into the data.. [2] The automatic labelling of such topics derived from social media poses however new challenges since topics may characterise novel events happening in the real world.. [3] Existing automatic topic labelling approaches which depend on external knowledge sources become less applicable here since relevant articles/concepts of the extracted topics may not exist in external sources.. [4] In this paper we propose to address the problem of automatic labelling of latent topics learned from Twitter as a summarisation problem.. [5] We introduce a framework which apply summarisation algorithms to generate topic labels.. [6] These algorithms are independent of external sources and only rely on the identification of dominant terms in documents related to the latent topic.. [7] We compare the efficiency of existing state of the art summarisation algorithms.. [8] Our results suggest that summarisation algorithms generate better topic labels which capture event-related context compared to the top- n terms returned by LDA..
