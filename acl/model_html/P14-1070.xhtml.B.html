[1] We experimented strategies to predict both MWE analysis and dependency structure, and tested them on the dependency version of French Treebank [ 1 ] , as instantiated in the SPMRL Shared Task [ 23 ].. [2] Our work focused on using an alternative representation of syntactically regular MWEs, which captures their syntactic internal structure.. [3] We obtain a system with comparable performance to that of previous works on this dataset, but which predicts both syntactic dependencies and the internal structure of MWEs.. [4] This can be useful for capturing the various degrees of semantic compositionality of MWEs.. [5] The main weakness of our system comes from the identification of regular MWEs, a property which is highly lexical.. [6] Our current use of external lexicons does not seem to suffice, and the use of data-driven external information to better cope with this identification can be envisaged..
