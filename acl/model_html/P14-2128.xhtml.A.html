[1] Modern statistical dependency parsers assign lexical heads to punctuations as well as words.. [2] Punctuation parsing errors lead to low parsing accuracy on words.. [3] In this work, we propose an alternative approach to addressing punctuation in dependency parsing.. [4] Rather than assigning lexical heads to punctuations, we treat punctuations as properties of their neighbouring words, used as features to guide the parser to build the dependency graph.. [5] Integrating our method with an arc-standard parser yields a 93.06 % unlabelled attachment score, which is the best accuracy by a single-model transition-based parser reported so far..
