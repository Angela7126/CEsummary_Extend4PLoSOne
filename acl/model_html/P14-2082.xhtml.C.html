[0] Today s event ordering research is heavily dependent on the annotated corpora available to it. [1] The corpora influence shared evaluations and drive algorithm development. [2] Partly due to this dependence, most research focuses on partial orderings of a document s events. [3] For instance, the TempEval competitions and the TimeBank only annotate small portions of the event graph. [4] They focus on the most salient events or on specific types of event pairs (e.g.,, only events in the same sentence. [5] Deeper temporal reasoners struggle with this sparsity because the entire temporal picture is not represented. [6] This paper proposes a new annotation process with a unique mechanism to force annotators to label connected graphs. [7] It generates 10 times more relations per document than the TimeBank and the corpus is larger than all current corpora.
