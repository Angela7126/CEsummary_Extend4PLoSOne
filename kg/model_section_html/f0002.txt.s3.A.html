[0] Knowledge flow spirals are formed when knowledge flows in a network. [1] A knowledge spirals consists of knowledge nodes (i.e, scientists) and two types of knowledge flow: [2] (1) External, knowledge flowing between nodes. [3] (2) internal, knowledge arising within a node. [4] A knowledge flow spiral can generate and generalize knowledge during the processing and recycling of knowledge. [5] Recoding the evolution of knowledge flows among authors needs a semantic space of three dimensions: article, author, and time. [6] Storing Knowledge requires a semantic space with three dimensions: knowledge level, area, and location. [7] An effective knowledge flow network enables a research team to be very powerful in generating new knowledge. [8] Selecting the appropriate higher-energy nodes to cooperate is a strategy to increase the efficiency of knowledge flow networking. [9] Encouraging unselfish cooperation is another strategy that helps a knowledge flow network reach its greatest effectiveness. [10] Some special knowledge spirals have effect in science.
