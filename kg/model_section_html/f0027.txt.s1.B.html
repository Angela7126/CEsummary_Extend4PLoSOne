[0] Scientists are trained early to keep careful records in their laboratory notebooks. [1] But data volumes are doubling every year in most areas of modern science and the analysis is becoming more and more complex, exceeding the capacity of the paper notebook. [2] Today, most scientists have replaced or enhanced their notebooks with desktop computers that record their results, provide a portal to the scientific literature, and link them to collaborators via email. [3] As data volumes grow, it is increasingly arduous to extract knowledge. [4] Scientists must labour to organize, sort and reduce the data, with each analysis step producing smaller data sets that eventually lead to the big picture. [5] In response to this data deluge, the systematic use of databases has become an integral part of the scientific process. [6] But there are few tools to properly visualize data across multiple scales and data sets.
