[0] Previous evaluation methods just focus on output, i.e., generated summaries. [1] Output is just one dimension, however, evaluating a summarization concerns multiple dimensions. [2] (1) Reader. [3] A summary should match the reader's interests, and concepts at the reader's cognitive level should be selected. [4] (2) Author. [5] A summarizer can understand the input representation better if it knows more about the author. [6] (3) Input. [7] Some representations are unnecessary for summarization, such as that are short and simple or the reader's uninterested representations. [8] (4) Summarizer. [9] a) Openness, because the closed system cannot make satisfied summarization without sharing knowledge with the writer. [10] b) Adaptability, i.e., it should improvable during use. [11] c) Interactivity, i.e., a summarizer should interact with readers to select appropriate representations. [12] (5) Output (summary), evaluating amount of core representation, coherence between representations, and completeness to satisfy the reader's interest. [13] (6) Usage, such as how many people using the summary and their attitudes.
