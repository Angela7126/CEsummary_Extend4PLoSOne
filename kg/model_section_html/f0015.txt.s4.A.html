[0] We cannot give definitions of mental qualities, because the concepts form a system that we fit as a whole to phenomena. [1] However, we can give approximate criteria for some specific properties relating humans or animals to beliefs and goals. [2] Intends. [3] We say that a machine intends to do something if we can regard it as believing that it will attempt to do it. [4] Tries. [5] This is important in understanding machines that have a variety of ways of achieving a goal including possibly ways that we don't know about. [6] Likes. . [7] As in 'A likes B', this involves A wanting B's welfare. [8] Self-consciousness. [9] Human self-consciousness involves at least the following: [10] 1) Facts about the person's body as a physical object. [11] 2) The ability to observe one's own mental processes and to form beliefs and wants about them. [12] 3) Facts about oneself as a having beliefs, wants, etc. among other similar beings. [13] Some of the above attributes of human self-consciousness are easy to program, while others are very difficult. [14] Therefore, many present and future programs can best be described as partially self-conscious.
