[0] Daniel Dennett, Tufts University philosopher, has proposed three attitudes aimed at understanding a system with which one interacts. [1] The first he calls the physical stance. [2] In this we look at the system in terms of its physical structure at various levels of organization. [3] The second is called the design stance. [4] In this we analyze something in terms of the purpose for which it is designed. [5] The third is called the intentional stance, and this is what we'll often need for understanding computer programs. [6] In this we try to understand the behavior of a system by ascribing to it beliefs, goals, intentions, likes and dislikes, and other mental qualities. [7] The intentional stance is most useful when it is the only way of expressing what we know about a system.
