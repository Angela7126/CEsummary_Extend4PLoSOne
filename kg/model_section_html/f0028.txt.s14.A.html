[0] Nowadays, science centers that curate and serve science data are emerging around next-generation science instruments. [1] Good metadata becomes central for data sharing among different disciplines and for data analysis and visualization tools. [2] There is also a convergence of the nascent-databases which focus primarily on the metadata issues and data interchange, and the traditional data management systems that have focused on managing and analyzing very large datasets. [3] And we believe the emphasis on extending database systems by unifying databases with programming languages so that one can either embed or link new object types into the data management system will enable this synthesis. [4] Three technical advances will be crucial to scientific analysis: [5] (1) extensive metadata and metadata standards; [6] (2) great analysis tools; [7] (3) set-oriented data parallelism access supported by new indexing schemes and new algorithms. [8] By interpreting these technicals, science centers will provide both the archives and the institutional infrastructure to develop these peta-scale archives and the algorithms and tools to analyze them.
