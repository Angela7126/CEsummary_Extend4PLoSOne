[0] Why are tabular databases so successful in commercial applications and such a flop in most scientific applications? [1] Scientific colleagues give following answers about why they do not use databases to manage their data: [2] 1) The cost for learning tools is worthless. [3] 2) They do not offer good visualization/plotting tools. [4] 3) I can handle my data volumes with my programming language. [5] 4) They do not support our data types. [6] 5) They do not support our access patterns (spatial, temporal, etc.). [7] 6) They were too slow. [8] 7) Once we loaded our data we could no longer manipulate the data using our standard application programs. [9] 8) They require an expensive guru to use. [10] All these are evidence that databases have to improve a lot before they are worth a second look.
