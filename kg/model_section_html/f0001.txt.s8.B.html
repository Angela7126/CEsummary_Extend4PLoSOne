[0] Automatic video summarization can help humans quickly know the key content in big video volumes. [1] It is important in video management, retrieval and browsing. [2] There are two fundamental classes of video summarization: [3] (1) focusing on still picture (static storyboard), a small collection of salient images extracted from video sources; [4] and, (2) focusing on moving pictures (moving storyboard), a collection of image sequences, and the corresponding audio abstract extracted from the original sequence, which results in a short video clip. [5] Summarizing a video requires machines to identify the essential characteristics of the video. [6] Video summarization concerns the simplification of motion. [7] The patterns of events, conversations, and behaviors should be characterized to get semantically meaningful summaries of complicated video contents [Tewfik, 1999]. [8] Current online movies contain subtitles, which provide a new condition for making summarization of videos through natural language processing. [9] Graphs are the generalization of various real networks in cyberspace (e.g., data structures), physical space (e.g., supply chains, material flow networks) and social space (e.g., social networks of things). [10] Summarizing data structures can provide more general data services. [11] Summarizing a large-scale graph of data is important to graph data management as it can render the patterns hidden in data [Navlakha, 2008] [Tian, 2008]. [12] The main purpose of summarizing pictures is to generate a small set of pictures from a large set of pictures according to interest. [13] Automatic summarization of pictures can be extended to include the solutions to the following issues: [14] (1) Generating a piece of text to represent a set of pictures. [15] (2) Selecting one picture to represent a set of related pictures. [16] (3) Generating a small network of pictures from a large set of related pictures. [17] (4) Generating a small network of texts according to a large set of pictures. [18] Modern cameras can generate pictures with time and location information, so the summarization of pictures can be carried out in a space of four dimensions: language, feature, time and location. [19] If we regard a text as a graph of words or sentences, text summarization can be regarded as a problem of summarizing a semantic link network. [20] A semantics-based approach to summarizing videos is to construct a semantic structure on videos by introducing semantic links into videos. [21] Discovering the semantic link networks that the pictures involved in can help automatic summarization. [22] The problem of summarizing texts, pictures, and videos can be generalized as one problem. [23] The form of summary can be a semantic link network of texts, pictures, audios, and videos.
