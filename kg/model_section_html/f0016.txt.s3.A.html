[0] Our ability to use common sense knowledge depends on being able to do common sense reasoning. [1] The logical system used in much artificial intelligent systems usually corresponds to a subset of first order logic. [2] However, deductive reasoning in mathematical logic has the monotonicity property. [3] Monotonicity property means that every sentence that was a deductive consequence of the original hypotheses is still a consequence of the enlarged set. [4] Ordinary human reasoning does not share this monotonicity property. [5] Therefore, several artificial intelligence researchers have pointed out that intelligent computer programs will have to reason nonmonotonically. [6] Actually, Deduction in mathematical logic can be supplemented by additional modes of nonmonotonic reasoning. [7] One such method is circumscription. [8] Circumscription describes the facts about a property or relation by requiring it to be true of a minimal set of objects. [9] For example, consider representing the facts about whether an object can fly in a database of common sense knowledge. [10] Circumscription allows us to express those objects can fly by adding a statement about it without removing existing statements in database, such as "bird can fly", "airplane can fly". [11] That is, we don't include negative statements in the database. [12] However, there may exists exceptions for some general statement, such as "penguins cannot fly" and penguins are birds. [13] Circumscription allows us to make the known exceptions and to provide for additional exceptions to be added later -- again without changing existing statements. [14] Nonmonotonic reasoning can also be regard as a communication convention. [15] For example, if a bird can fly the fact need not be mentioned, but if the bird can't fly and it is relevant, then the fact must be mentioned.
