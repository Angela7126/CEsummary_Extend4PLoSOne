[0] Most people do not know how machines work internally, but they do know machines' external behaviors if they are able to use these machines. [1] As computers and machines become more and more sophisticated, people have to know more complex external information about them in order to use them. [2] We will find that ascribing little thoughts to machines will be increasingly useful in understanding how to get the most good out of them. [3] We will find ourselves using psychological words like 'knows', 'thinks', and 'wants' in referring to machines. [4] Therefore, the questions of whether machines can think and what kind of thoughts they can have become ever more pertinent. [5] Is this an all-or-nothing question, or can we say that some machines do some of these things and not others, or that they do them to some extent?
