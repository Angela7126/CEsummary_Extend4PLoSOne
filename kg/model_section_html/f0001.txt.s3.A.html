[0] Human language use and understanding has some basic characteristics. [1] These characteristics lead to following principles of language use: [2] (1) Separation principle, i.e, grammar and semantics, knowledge and representation, representation and its summaries are separated. [3] Separation principles lead to the natural obstacle of summarization. [4] (2) Social selection principle, that is, the suitable summarizer for a representation is rendered by the social network of its writers and readers [5] (3) Knowledge Selection princile, that is, the person sharing the represented knowledge and the knowledge for representation is suitable for summarization. [6] Therefore, authors who commonly cited a representation are suitable summarizers, and authors are the best persons to summarize their own representations. [7] (4) Relativity principle, (5) moderate principle and (6) dynamicity principle are about the satisfactory of summaries. [8] These three principles show that the satisfied degree of a representation's summary varies with its readers and time. [9] (7) Openness principle say that a satisfied summary cannot be reached in a closed system, therefore establishing static criteria for evaluating is unnecessary. [10] Above basic characteristics and principles indicate some summarization strategies. [11] (1) Using summaries composed by people who have rich social relations with original authors. [12] (2) Adapting to readers' interests. [13] (3) Making summarization through human-machine interaction. [14] (4) Enabling different summaries to cooperate and compete with each other for impact at multiple dimensions. [15] (5) Transforming summarization process into the problem of searching suitable persons or summaries in social networks of authors, readers, summarizers, representations and links. [16] (6) Integrating background of representations into summarizers.
