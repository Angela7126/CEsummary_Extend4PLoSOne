[0] We shall discuss various areas of common sense knowledge. [1] 1) The most salient common sense knowledge concerns situations that change in time as a result of events. [2] The most important events are actions, and for a program to plan intelligently, it must be able to determine the effects of its own actions. [3] MYCIN doesn't have such common sense knowledge, so its treatment sometimes really needs plan and that lack of planning ability limits MYCIN's utility. [4] 2) Various formalisms are used in artificial intelligence for representing facts about the effects of actions and other events. [5] However, most systems give the effects of an event in a situation by describing a new situation that results from the event. [6] This doesn't cover the important case of concurrent events and actions. [7] We need to invent a formalism that will conveniently express people's common sense knowledge about concurrent events. [8] 3) We need to handle the kind of partial knowledge people ordinarily have. [9] Partial knowledge means the ability to make predictions about the object's whole situation with its partial information. [10] 4) The ability to represent and use knowledge about knowledge is often required for intelligent behavior. [11] For example, an advanced MYCIN might need to reason that Dr. Smith knows about cholera, because he is a specialist in tropical medicine. [12] 5) A program that must co-operate or compete with people or other programs must be able to represent information about their knowledge, beliefs, goals, likes and dislikes, intentions and abilities. [13] 6) Common sense includes much knowledge whose domain overlaps that of the exact sciences but differs from it epistemologically. [14] For example, scientific physics is imbedded in common sense physics, because it is common sense physics that tells us what the equation s = 0.5gt^(2) means. [15] Whether the common sense world can be represented adequately by production rules is doubtful, since each rule match facts about specific objects.
