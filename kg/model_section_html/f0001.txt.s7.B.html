[0] The summary with pictures is more attractive than the text-only summary. [1] Summaries incorporating pictures can enhance reader's short-term and long-term memory. [2] One advantage of incorporating pictures into summary is that pictures provide a different bridge between language units (e.g., sentences). [3] There could be different ways to arrange pictures in displays but picture should be selected according to the core sentences and arranged near the core sentences according to the semantic locality principle [Zhuge, 2010]. [4] (1) How to select appropriate pictures? [5] The semantic link networks of texts, pictures, tags and users indicate a kind of social semantics of picture usage and thus provide the ground for selection. [6] (2) How to organize pictures and texts? [7] a) Use pictures to replace the corresponding representations in the original text, to summarize the rest representation, and to organize the summary according to the original structure. [8] b) Select and use pictures to replace the texts in summary. [9] c) Select and insert pictures into summary at appropriate places. [10] d) Classifying pictures from multiple dimensions including time and location, which help distinguish pictures of different dimensions so that appropriate pictures can be selected to match the text in the summary. [11] e) Construct a semantic link network of pictures, tags and language representations in relevant texts as the summary. [12] (3) How to identify events in pictures and link them to appropriate texts? [13] The strategy is to make use of sensors and create semantic links between pictures and texts by detecting common projections at physical and social dimensions. [14] So, the techniques of text summary can be extended to the construction of semantic link network and image retrieval [Gudivada and Raghavan, 1995] [15] According to the efficiency principle and regional principle, the radius of the network should be small. [16] The radius can be defined as the maximum length of link chain from the center (determined by core nodes) to any node.
