[0] Not all experiments will experience exponential growth. [1] There are two trends in science today, scaling up and scaling out. [2] In scaling-up model, scientists are building billion-dollar facilities, which will not be easily leapfrogged. [3] And once such facilities are switched on, they will produce roughly the same amount of data each year -- merely linear growth. [4] But in the scaling-out model, experiments that deploy an array of small instruments can exploit the coming explosion in cheaper commodity technology. [5] Considering these two trends, scientists in 2020 will continue to work in an exponential world.
