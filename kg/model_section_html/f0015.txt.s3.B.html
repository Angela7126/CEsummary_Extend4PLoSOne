[1] Anthropomorphism is the ascription of human characteristics to things not human. [2] The reason for ascribing mental qualities and mental processes to machines is the same as for ascribing them to other people. [3] It helps understand what they will do, how our actions will affect them, how to compare them with ourselves and how to design them. [4] Researchers in artificial intelligence (AI) are interested in the use of mental terms to describe machines for two reasons. [5] First we want to provide machines with theories of knowledge and belief so they can reason about what their users know, don't know, and want. [6] Second what the user knows about the machine can often best be expressed using mental terms. [7] It's difficult to be rigorous about whether a machine really 'knows', 'thinks', etc., because we're hard put to define these things. [8] Beliefs and goals are ascribed in accordance with the the principle of rationality. [9] Our object is to account for as much behavior as possible by saying the machine or person or animal does what it thinks will achieve its goals. [10] The language must be able to express the information our program can actually get about a person's or machine's 'state of mind'.
