[0] Generally, citation is a kind of representation of selection and language use. [1] Citation is a basic semantic link of constructing a complex text. [2] Therefore, it is necessary to understand citation when studying summarization. [3] Explicit citation is often used in scientific papers and books, in form of '[reference number]' or '(author, year)'. [4] Hyperlink of the Web is a kind of explicit citation that freely complements, explains, or extends the content of the current Web pages. [5] Implicit citation is used in free texts and literature works. [6] Some implicit citations have mark words such as "someone says" and according to "someone's opinion". [7] Some implicit citations just reuse others' sentences or clauses without any mark word. [8] Summarizing various citations can reach a notion of general citation. [9] Citations form the intention and extension of a representation. [10] The extension of representation A consists of all representations that cite A and are cited by A. [11] The core representation renders the core idea of a representation. [12] The intention of representation is indicated by core representations and by citation from other representations. [13] A representation can be composed by a set of representations. [14] Dictionaries explain words in texts, so they can be regarded as the basic implicit citations to all texts. [15] From this point of view, any text has a set of basic citations. [16] The basic behaviors of summarization include emerging, selecting, citing (explicitly or implicitly), and organizing representations according to requirement and motivation.
