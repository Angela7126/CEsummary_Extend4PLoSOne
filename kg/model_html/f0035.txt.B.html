[0] The primary goal of the World Wide Telescope is to make Astronomers more productive and to allow them to better understand their data. [1] But, it is also an archetype for the evolution of Computational Science from its simulation roots to the broader field of capturing, organizing, analyzing, exploring, and visualizing scientific data. [2] The World Wide Telescope is a prototype for this new role, but similar trends are happening in genomics, in economics, in ecology, and in most other sciences. [3] This transformation poses interesting challenges to the database community which will have to deal with huge datasets, with richer datatypes, and with much more complex queries. [4] Federating the archives is a good test of our distributed systems technologies like web services and distributed object stores. [5] The WWT poses a challenge to the data mining community since these datasets are so huge and the data has such high dimensionality. [6] It is an excellent place to compare and evaluate new datamining algorithms, since the data is public. [7] It is a challenge to statisticians to develop algorithms that run fast on very large datasets. [8] The WWT also poses the challenge of making it easy to visually explore the data, posing queries in natural ways, and seeing the answers in intuitive formats. [9] Last but perhaps most important, the WWT can be a valuable resource to teach the new astronomy and also to teach computational science.
