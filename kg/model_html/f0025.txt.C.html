[0] Logical AI develops computer programs that represent what they know about the world primarily by logical formulas and decide what to do primarily by logical reasoning|including nonmonotonic logical reasoning. [1] It is convenient to use logical sentences and terms whose meaning depends on context. [2] The reasons for this are similar to what causes human language to use context dependent meanings. [3] This note gives elements of some of the formalisms to which we have been led. [4] Fuller treatments are in [McC93], [Guh91] and [MB94] and the references cited in the Web page [Buv95]. [5] The first main idea is to make contexts first class objects in the logic and use the formula ist(c,p) to assert that the proposition p is true in the context c. [6] A second idea is to formalize how propositions true in one context transform when they are moved to different but related contexts. [7] An ability to transcend the outermost context is needed to give computer programs the ability to reason about the totality of all they have thought about so far [McC96].
