[0] The idea is to chip a piece out of the problem of creativity by defining a creative solution to a problem relative to the functions and predicates used in posing the problem. [1] The simplification comes from not talking about the creativity of the problem solver but only about the creativity of the solution. [2] Definition (informal): [3] A solution to a problem is creative if it involves concepts not present in statement of the problem and the general knowledge surrounding it. [4] Don't identify creativity with difficulty although they are usually correlated. [5] Example: [6] The mutilated checkerboard problem. [7] We also consider how to express concisely the idea of a solution. [8] Whether the expression is adequate is relative to the knowledge and ability of the person or program to which the idea is expressed.
