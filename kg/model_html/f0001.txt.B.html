[0] Summarization is an open representation of representation, in diverse forms, from multiple dimensions, and through interactions in multiple spaces. [1] The basic interactions include selecting, citing and organizing representations according to requirement and motivation. [2] Texts have been the major means to reflect the evolution of human society. [3] It is necessary to explore automatic summarization of texts expanding rapidly in cyberspace. [4] However, existing approaches are empirical, mainly relying on statistics, structure and linguistic analysis while neglecting the nature of representation and understanding. [5] This paper summarizes previous approaches and explores the fundamental theory and method for summarization from multiple dimensions. [6] The core viewpoint is that summarization is carried out with various interactions involved in human, machine, and various representations including text, picture, and video. [7] Writers and readers represent mainly through explicit or implicit citations. [8] Studying the summarization of pictures, videos and graphs reaches a general summarization method. [9] It is hard for automatic summarization systems to realize human-level summarization as it concerns the essential natural and social differences between human and the systems. [10] Putting summarization into a human-machine-nature symbiotic network is a way to make a breakthrough. [11] A significant progress of summarization research relies on an innovative summarization of philosophy, psychology, linguistics, cognitive science, neuroscience, physics, computer science, and artificial intelligence from multiple dimensions. [12] This work is an attempt to help broaden the scope of summarization research and inspire cross-disciplinary research to develop this area.
