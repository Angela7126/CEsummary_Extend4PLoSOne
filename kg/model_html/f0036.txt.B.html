[0] Data stores will become huge. [1] Our biggest challenge is to make it easy to access and manage them. [2] Automating all the tasks of data organization, accesses, and protection. [3] Disk technology is overtaking tapes, but at the same time disks are morphing into tape-like devices with primarily sequential access to optimize the use of disk arms. [4] Meanwhile, RAM improvements encourage us to build machines with massive main memory. [5] Indeed, the main change to Amdahl's balanced system law is that alpha (=MIPS/RAM size) is rising from 1 to 10. [6] Network bandwidth is improving at a rate that challenges many of our design assumptions. [7] LAN/SAN software is being streamlined so it is no longer the bottleneck. [8] This may well allow a re -centralization of computing. [9] Still, data caching is an important optimization. [10] Disk caching still follows the 5-minute random rule and the one-minute sequential rule. [11] Web caching encourages designs that simply cache all pages.
